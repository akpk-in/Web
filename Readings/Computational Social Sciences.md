01. Bit by Bit: Social Research in the Digital Age by Matthew J. Salganik/This book provides an excellent introduction to computational social science methods and their applications.
02. Computational Social Science: Discovery and Prediction by R. Michael Alvarez/A comprehensive overview of computational methods in social sciences.
03. Introduction to Agent-Based Modeling by Uri Wilensky and William Rand Focuses on agent-based modeling, a key technique in computational social science.
04. Text as Data: A New Framework for Machine Learning and the Social Sciences by Justin Grimmer, Margaret E. Roberts, and Brandon M. Stewart Explores text analysis methods for social science research.
05. Big Data and Social Science: A Practical Guide to Methods and Tools edited by Ian Foster et al./Covers various computational methods and tools for social science research.
06. Networks, Crowds, and Markets: Reasoning About a Highly Connected World by David Easley and Jon Kleinberg Explores network analysis in social contexts.
07. Doing Computational Social Science: A Practical Introduction by John McLevey Provides hands-on guidance for applying computational methods to social research.
08. The Oxford Handbook of Computational Social Science edited by Michael Alvarez/A comprehensive resource covering various aspects of computational social science.
09. Advances in Social Simulation edited by Bruce Edmonds and Ruth Meyer Focuses on social simulation techniques and their applications.
10. Computational Sociology by Flaminio Squazzoni Specifically addresses computational methods in sociology.
